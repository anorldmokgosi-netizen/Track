Signature Destination

Okavango Delta

The world's largest inland delta, a UNESCO World Heritage Site where waterways create a unique oasis in the Kalahari Desert. Experience mokoro excursions, walking safaris, and incredible birdlife.

  • UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Mokoro (dugout canoe) safaris
  • Exceptional birdwatching (400+ species)
  • Big Five game viewing
Best: May-October Water-based safaris Small group focus

Predator Paradise

Moremi Game Reserve

The first protected area in the Okavango Delta, known for its diverse habitats and exceptional predator sightings. Explore forests, floodplains, and lagoons teeming with wildlife.

  • Exceptional leopard sightings
  • African wild dog territory
  • Diverse ecosystems
  • Year-round wildlife viewing
Best: Year-round Predator hotspot Forest & floodplain

Elephant Kingdom

Chobe National Park

Home to Africa's largest elephant population, Chobe offers spectacular river safaris and dense wildlife concentrations along the Chobe Riverfront.

  • Largest elephant herds in Africa
  • Spectacular river cruises
  • Diverse habitats (riverine, forest, floodplain)
  • Excellent lion and buffalo viewing
Best: May-October River safaris Elephant capital
View Chobe Safaris
Predator Arena

Savuti Marsh

A legendary region within Chobe National Park known for dramatic predator-prey interactions, mysterious water channels, and iconic baobab trees.

  • Famous lion vs elephant interactions
  • Iconic baobab-studded landscapes
  • Mysterious Savuti Channel
  • Exceptional dry season viewing
Best: May-October Dramatic wildlife Unique landscapes

When to Visit Botswana

Plan your safari according to Botswana's distinct seasons

Dry Season (May - October)

Peak safari season for wildlife viewing

  • Excellent wildlife viewing around water sources
  • Minimal mosquitoes and malaria risk
  • Pleasant daytime temperatures
  • Clear skies and great photography conditions
  • Okavango Delta at peak water levels (Jun-Aug)

Best During Dry Season:

Chobe Riverfront Savuti Marsh Moremi Game Reserve Central Kalahari (Jul-Oct)

Green Season (November - April)

Lush landscapes and newborn animals

  • Lush green landscapes and flowering plants
  • Birding paradise with migratory species
  • Lower rates and fewer tourists
  • Spectacular thunderstorms and dramatic skies
  • Newborn animals (Dec-Feb)

Best During Green Season:

Okavango Delta Central Kalahari (Dec-Apr) Makgadikgadi Pans Nxai Pan National Park

Botswana's Iconic Wildlife

Discover the incredible animals you can encounter on safari

African Elephant

Botswana has the world's largest elephant population, with over 130,000 elephants roaming freely.

Best viewing: Year-round in Chobe, dry season in Savuti

African Lion

From the famous Savuti lions to the black-maned Kalahari lions, Botswana offers exceptional lion viewing.

Best viewing: Dry season in Savuti & Moremi

Leopard

Moremi and Khwai are renowned for leopard sightings, with these elusive cats often seen in trees.

Best viewing: Year-round in Moremi & Khwai

African Wild Dog

Botswana has one of Africa's largest populations of these endangered, highly social predators.

Best viewing: Denning season (May-Jul) in Moremi

Cape Buffalo

Large herds can be seen throughout northern Botswana, particularly in Chobe and Moremi.

Best viewing: Dry season around water sources

Birdlife

Over 500 species recorded, including rare slaty egrets, Pel's fishing owls, and carmine bee-eaters.

Best viewing: Green season (Nov-Apr) for migrants

Cheetah

Open areas of the Kalahari and Savuti provide perfect habitat for these speedy predators.

Best viewing: Dry season in open grasslands

Hippopotamus

Abundant in the Okavango Delta and Chobe River, often seen in pods during boat safaris.

Best viewing: Year-round in waterways
